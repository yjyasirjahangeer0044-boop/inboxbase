# Brevo vs Klaviyo: The Better Choice for E-commerce?

Klaviyo built its reputation as the email marketing tool for serious ecommerce. Built specifically for Shopify, Klaviyo offers deep behavioral segmentation, advanced predictive analytics, and a feature set that brand-conscious DTC companies love. The problem: Klaviyo's pricing rises aggressively with list size, and many ecommerce stores discover they're paying $500 to $2,000+ per month before they've crossed $1M in annual revenue.

Brevo offers a credible alternative for ecommerce — Shopify integration via PushOwl, WooCommerce native plugin, abandoned cart workflows, behavior-based segmentation, and SMS/WhatsApp marketing all included. The question isn't whether Brevo can replace Klaviyo (it can for most stores); it's whether Klaviyo's premium features justify the premium price for your specific business.

## Pricing: Where Klaviyo's Premium Bites

Klaviyo prices by active profiles (contacts who can receive emails). A growing store can easily 5x its costs without sending a single additional email.

For a store with 10,000 active profiles sending 4 campaigns per month:

| Plan | Klaviyo | Brevo |
|------|---------|-------|
| Email only | $180/month | $18/month |
| Email + SMS | $235/month (SMS bolts on) | $18/month (SMS included) |

For 50,000 active profiles:

| Plan | Klaviyo | Brevo |
|------|---------|-------|
| Email only | $720/month | $65/month (Standard 20K tier) |
| Email + SMS | $850/month | $65/month (SMS included) |

For 100,000 active profiles:

| Plan | Klaviyo | Brevo |
|------|---------|-------|
| Email only | $1,540/month | $129/month (Standard 100K tier) |

At scale, Brevo costs roughly **10x to 12x less** than Klaviyo for comparable email volume. The math becomes brutal as your list grows.

## What Klaviyo Does Better

Honest assessment: Klaviyo has genuine advantages worth understanding before deciding.

**Predictive analytics:**
Klaviyo's predictive lifetime value (CLV) calculations, churn risk modeling, and next-order prediction are best-in-class. These data points power smart segmentation (e.g., "high CLV customers showing churn signals"). Brevo's segmentation is good but less predictive.

**Shopify-native depth:**
Klaviyo's Shopify integration runs deeper than Brevo's PushOwl-based connection. Klaviyo syncs more product attributes, more customer behaviors, and integrates more tightly with Shopify's checkout extensions.

**Pre-built ecommerce flows:**
Klaviyo's template library for ecommerce flows (browse abandonment, post-purchase, win-back, replenishment) is extensive and based on aggregated data across thousands of stores. Brevo's templates cover the basics but with less ecommerce-specific tuning.

**Benchmark data:**
Klaviyo publishes industry benchmarks across thousands of stores, giving you reference points for what "good" looks like in your vertical. Brevo's benchmark publishing is less ecommerce-focused.

**Reviews integration:**
Klaviyo integrates natively with Shopify reviews apps for triggering review requests at optimal timing. Brevo handles this via custom workflows or Zapier.

**Brand recognition:**
For DTC brands working with consultants or agencies, Klaviyo is the "expected" choice. Switching to Brevo can require defending the decision.

## What Brevo Does Better

**Pricing scaling:**
Already covered, but worth repeating: Brevo's send-volume pricing dramatically outperforms Klaviyo's contact-based pricing as you grow.

**Multi-channel from day one:**
Brevo includes SMS, WhatsApp, web push, and live chat in a single dashboard. Klaviyo bolts SMS onto its email infrastructure at additional cost. WhatsApp isn't available natively in Klaviyo.

**Transactional email included:**
Brevo's transactional email API is excellent and bundled with marketing plans. Klaviyo doesn't have a comparable offering — you'd use a separate service like Postmark.

**Free plan generosity:**
Brevo's free plan supports 100,000 contacts and 300 emails per day. Klaviyo's free plan supports 250 active profiles. The free tier serves dramatically different segments.

**WooCommerce integration:**
Brevo's WooCommerce plugin is one of the best in the WordPress ecosystem. Klaviyo has WooCommerce support but it's not as deep or as well-maintained.

**Simpler interface:**
Brevo's interface is more approachable for first-time users. Klaviyo's depth comes with a steeper learning curve — many stores hire Klaviyo consultants for implementation.

**B2B marketing:**
Klaviyo is ecommerce-focused; its workflows assume retail customer behavior. Brevo serves ecommerce + B2B + service businesses, which matters if you span multiple business models.

## Segmentation Comparison

Both platforms support behavior-based segmentation, but with different depths.

**Klaviyo:**
- Predictive analytics: CLV, churn risk, next purchase date
- Deep event tracking: every page view, click, product interaction
- Dynamic segments with complex multi-condition logic
- Built-in flows tied to specific Shopify events

**Brevo:**
- Behavior segments based on email engagement, purchase history, contact attributes
- Custom event tracking via API or plugin
- Dynamic segments with multi-condition filters
- Cross-channel segments (email engagement + SMS engagement + browsing)

Klaviyo's predictive depth is real and valuable for stores willing to pay for it. Brevo's segmentation is sufficient for the 80% of ecommerce use cases — abandoned carts, repeat purchasers, lapsed customers, high-value buyers.

## Automation Capabilities

Both offer visual workflow builders with conditional branching, delays, and multi-step sequences.

**Klaviyo's edge:**
- More ecommerce-specific automation templates
- Tighter Shopify event integration for granular triggers
- Better A/B testing within flows (multi-variant testing)
- Built-in send time optimization (Brevo has this too on Standard+)

**Brevo's edge:**
- Multi-channel branching (email → SMS in same flow)
- Cleaner interface for non-technical users
- More affordable at scale
- WhatsApp inclusion on Professional plan

For most stores running standard ecommerce automation (welcome series, abandoned cart, post-purchase, win-back), Brevo handles all of these well. For stores building dozens of complex segmented flows, Klaviyo's depth helps.

## Deliverability

Both platforms invest heavily in deliverability infrastructure. Independent testing places both in the top tier of email service providers.

Klaviyo's reputation: consistently strong, with shared IP pools optimized for ecommerce sending patterns.

Brevo's reputation: equally strong, with shared IP pools that serve a broader sender mix (ecommerce, B2B, transactional). Dedicated IPs available on Professional plan as add-on.

**Verdict: roughly equivalent for properly authenticated senders.**

## When to Choose Klaviyo

Klaviyo makes sense if:
- You're scaling a high-revenue ecommerce business ($3M+ annual)
- You have dedicated marketing resources or a Klaviyo consultant
- Predictive analytics drive your campaign decisions
- You're deeply embedded in the Shopify ecosystem
- You can afford $500+/month for marketing software
- Brand recognition matters to your agency or consultants

## When to Choose Brevo

Brevo makes sense if:
- You're an early-stage or mid-size ecommerce store (under $3M annual)
- You're cost-conscious about scaling marketing tool spend
- You want SMS, WhatsApp, and email in one tool
- You run WooCommerce instead of (or alongside) Shopify
- You need transactional email + marketing combined
- You're a hybrid business (ecommerce + service or B2B)

## The Migration Path

Switching from Klaviyo to Brevo is straightforward:

1. **Export Klaviyo data** — contacts, lists, segments, historical event data
2. **Import to Brevo** — Brevo's contact importer handles Klaviyo exports
3. **Recreate critical flows** in Brevo's automation builder (templates speed this up)
4. **Set up Shopify integration** via PushOwl/Brevo app
5. **Authenticate your sending domain** (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
6. **Test campaigns** to a small segment
7. **Cut over** and cancel Klaviyo

Most migrations complete in 2 to 4 weeks. For a store paying Klaviyo $720+/month, the cost savings cover migration effort within the first billing cycle.
