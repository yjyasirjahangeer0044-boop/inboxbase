# Brevo vs HubSpot: The Honest 2026 Comparison for Small Business

HubSpot built one of the most comprehensive sales and marketing platforms in existence — and charges accordingly. For small businesses staring at HubSpot's pricing pages and feeling sticker shock, Brevo offers a credible alternative that covers 70 to 80% of HubSpot's core marketing functionality at a small fraction of the cost.

This comparison goes deep on what each platform actually delivers, where HubSpot's higher cost is justified, and where Brevo wins decisively. The verdict depends heavily on your business stage and feature requirements.

## Pricing: The Most Dramatic Gap

HubSpot's pricing structure has multiple "hubs" (Marketing, Sales, Service, CMS, Operations), each priced independently. For just the Marketing Hub:

| Plan | HubSpot Marketing | Brevo |
|------|-------------------|-------|
| Free | $0 (limited) | $0 (300 emails/day, 100K contacts) |
| Entry paid | $20/month (Starter, 1K contacts) | $9/month (Starter, 5K emails) |
| Mid tier | $890/month (Professional, 2K contacts) | $18/month (Standard, full automation) |
| Premium | $3,600/month (Enterprise, 10K contacts) | $499/month (Professional, 150K emails) |

For a business with 10,000 contacts, HubSpot Marketing Professional costs approximately $1,690/month (after contact tier upgrades). Brevo Standard delivers comparable email marketing for $18/month. That's roughly **94x cost difference**.

HubSpot's value proposition includes far more than just email — CRM, sales pipeline, service desk, CMS — and that's the honest defense for the pricing. But many businesses paying HubSpot's premium prices end up using only the email marketing features, in which case they're dramatically overpaying.

## What HubSpot Includes That Brevo Doesn't

HubSpot is genuinely more comprehensive than Brevo in several areas:

**Sales pipeline and CRM depth:**
HubSpot's CRM is one of the strongest free CRMs available, with deep deal tracking, contact scoring, sales sequences, and forecasting. Brevo's CRM is basic by comparison — adequate for tracking deals but not for running a full sales operation.

**Customer service tools:**
HubSpot Service Hub includes ticket management, live chat, knowledge base, and customer feedback tools. Brevo has live chat but no full service desk functionality.

**Content management system:**
HubSpot CMS lets you build entire websites with marketing-integrated forms and personalization. Brevo doesn't offer a CMS — you'd pair it with WordPress, Webflow, or Shopify.

**Marketing operations automation:**
HubSpot Operations Hub provides advanced data syncing, custom workflow logic, and programmable automation. Brevo's automation is robust but less programmable.

**Reporting depth:**
HubSpot's reporting includes multi-touch attribution, custom dashboards, and predictive analytics. Brevo's reporting covers email and SMS metrics deeply but lacks HubSpot's cross-channel attribution.

**Native sales sequences:**
HubSpot includes structured sales email sequences for 1:1 outreach with tracking. Brevo focuses on marketing automation rather than sales prospecting workflows.

## What Brevo Does Better Than HubSpot

**Email send pricing:**
Brevo's send-volume pricing is dramatically more economical for growing lists. HubSpot's contact-based pricing penalizes you for growing your audience even if you're not sending more emails.

**SMS and WhatsApp:**
Brevo includes native SMS marketing across all plans and WhatsApp on Professional. HubSpot offers SMS through SurveyMonkey integration but it's clunky.

**Transactional email:**
Brevo's transactional email API is excellent and included on every plan. HubSpot doesn't have a comparable transactional offering.

**Free plan generosity:**
Brevo's free plan supports 100,000 contacts and 300 emails per day. HubSpot's free plan caps marketing emails at 2,000 per month and includes severe contact limits at higher tiers.

**Deliverability infrastructure:**
Both have good deliverability, but Brevo's reputation for inbox placement is consistently strong across independent reviews. HubSpot's deliverability is solid but its higher-tier plans don't include dedicated IPs without expensive add-ons.

**Cost of scaling:**
A business going from 5,000 contacts to 50,000 contacts on Brevo sees minimal pricing change (still on Standard plan if email volume stays similar). On HubSpot, the same growth could push monthly cost from $890 to $3,000+.

## Ease of Use

Both platforms are well-designed but optimized for different users.

HubSpot is built for marketing teams with dedicated resources. The interface assumes you're using multiple hubs together, and the learning curve for full platform mastery is significant — many companies hire HubSpot consultants for implementation.

Brevo is built for small teams (or solo founders) who need marketing automation without dedicated specialists. The interface is simpler and gets you sending campaigns in 20 to 30 minutes from a fresh account.

**Verdict:** HubSpot for marketing teams of 5+ with budget for training; Brevo for teams of 1 to 5 prioritizing speed and simplicity.

## Marketing Automation Comparison

Both platforms offer visual workflow builders. Some differences:

**HubSpot:**
- Deeper conditional branching options
- Built-in CRM-aware triggers (deal stage changes, lead scoring updates)
- Integration with sales sequences
- Goal-based workflow optimization
- Stronger lead scoring with AI assistance

**Brevo:**
- Cleaner interface for visual workflow building
- Easier to learn for first-time users
- Faster setup for basic sequences (welcome series, abandoned cart, post-purchase)
- Multi-channel native (email + SMS + WhatsApp from same workflow)
- More forgiving for non-technical marketers

For sophisticated B2B marketing operations, HubSpot's automation is more powerful. For B2C and small-business marketing, Brevo's automation is faster to implement and covers 90% of common scenarios.

## Customer Support

**HubSpot:**
- Free plan: community forums, chat with bot
- Paid plans: email and chat support
- Professional and Enterprise: phone support
- Premium: dedicated success manager

**Brevo:**
- Free plan: email support
- Starter, Standard: email + chat
- Professional: phone support included
- Enterprise: dedicated account manager

HubSpot's support is consistently rated excellent, particularly the dedicated success manager program at higher tiers. Brevo's support is strong but less hand-holding — the platform assumes you're more self-sufficient.

## Integration Ecosystem

HubSpot's app marketplace has over 1,500 integrations — one of the largest in SaaS. The ecosystem reflects HubSpot's market position as a hub for entire marketing stacks.

Brevo has 150+ native integrations plus Zapier (5,000+ apps) plus a strong REST API. The native count is smaller, but the API and Zapier coverage closes the gap for most use cases.

**Verdict:** HubSpot wins on native integration breadth. Brevo wins on API flexibility and Zapier-supported workflows.

## Who Should Choose HubSpot

HubSpot makes sense when:
- You have a marketing budget over $1,500/month dedicated to platform tools
- You need integrated CRM + sales + marketing + service in one system
- You run sophisticated B2B marketing with long sales cycles
- You have or can hire a HubSpot administrator
- Multi-touch attribution and predictive analytics drive your decisions
- Your team values consolidated tooling over best-of-breed

For mid-market and enterprise companies fitting this profile, HubSpot's premium pricing is genuinely justified.

## Who Should Choose Brevo

Brevo makes sense when:
- You're a small business, solo founder, or early-stage startup
- Your primary need is email + automation + maybe SMS
- You don't need a deep CRM (or can use a separate one like Pipedrive)
- You're cost-conscious and want predictable pricing as you grow
- You value speed of implementation over feature depth
- You send under 200,000 emails per month

For roughly 70% of small and mid-size businesses, Brevo delivers what they actually use at a fraction of HubSpot's cost.

## The Hybrid Approach

Some businesses use both: HubSpot's free CRM (which is genuinely excellent at no cost) paired with Brevo for email marketing. This combination gives you HubSpot's sales pipeline and contact management plus Brevo's economical email infrastructure. The integration via Zapier or HubSpot's marketplace is straightforward.

This hybrid stack costs roughly $18 to $50/month versus $900+ for HubSpot's Marketing Professional plan. For most growing businesses, the savings are too large to ignore.

## Final Verdict

If you're staring at HubSpot's pricing and feeling skeptical, your instincts are correct. For small businesses, HubSpot Marketing Hub is dramatically overpriced relative to what most businesses actually need. **Brevo delivers the core email marketing and automation value at a small fraction of the cost**, with the caveat that you'll need separate tools (or HubSpot's free CRM) for advanced sales and service workflows.

For marketing teams with $1,500+/month budgets, mid-market B2B operations, and a need for tightly integrated sales+marketing+service tooling, HubSpot is still worth the premium. For everyone else, Brevo is the obvious choice.
