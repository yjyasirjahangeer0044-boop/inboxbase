# Brevo vs ConvertKit: Which Platform Suits Creators Better?

ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit in some markets) built its reputation as the email marketing tool for creators — bloggers, YouTubers, course creators, newsletter writers, and digital product entrepreneurs. The platform's clean interface, creator-focused features, and active community gave it dominant mindshare in the creator economy.

Brevo competes for the same audience but with a different value proposition: broader functionality (email + SMS + WhatsApp + transactional + CRM) at significantly lower pricing. For creators trying to choose between them, the answer depends on whether you value ConvertKit's creator-specific features or Brevo's multi-channel breadth and economics.

## What ConvertKit Does Better

**Creator-focused interface:**
ConvertKit's UX is built around how creators think — broadcast emails, sequences, automations, tags. The mental model maps cleanly to creator workflows (newsletter, course launch sequences, evergreen funnels).

**Landing page builder:**
ConvertKit's landing page builder is creator-optimized with templates for lead magnets, newsletter signups, and product launches. Brevo has landing pages on Standard plan but the templates are less creator-specific.

**Creator Network:**
ConvertKit's Recommendations feature lets creators recommend each other's newsletters, generating cross-promotion within the platform. This is unique to ConvertKit and valuable for newsletter growth.

**Tip jar and paid newsletters:**
ConvertKit supports tip jars and paid newsletter subscriptions natively. Brevo doesn't have built-in paid newsletter monetization.

**Customer success focus:**
ConvertKit invests heavily in helping creators succeed — extensive education, active community, frequent webinars. The platform's culture is creator-first.

**Tagging system:**
ConvertKit's tag-based audience management is intuitive for creators who think in terms of interests and behaviors rather than lists. Brevo has tags but the platform's mental model leans more toward lists and segments.

## What Brevo Does Better

**Pricing at every tier:**
Brevo's pricing is dramatically more economical, especially as subscriber lists grow. The savings compound month after month.

**Multi-channel marketing:**
Brevo includes SMS, WhatsApp, web push, and live chat. ConvertKit is email-only. For creators wanting to diversify beyond email (which is increasingly important as email opens decline), Brevo provides infrastructure ConvertKit lacks.

**Free plan generosity:**
Brevo's free plan supports 100,000 subscribers. ConvertKit's free plan caps at 1,000. For early creators, Brevo lets you grow significantly before paying anything.

**Transactional email:**
Brevo includes transactional email APIs for sending password resets, order confirmations, course access emails. ConvertKit doesn't have transactional email infrastructure — you'd pair it with Postmark or SendGrid.

**Automation depth:**
Brevo's automation builder offers more conditional branching and multi-trigger workflows than ConvertKit. For creators running sophisticated funnels (launch sequences, segmented onboarding, behavior-based follow-ups), Brevo provides more flexibility.

**CRM integration:**
Brevo includes a basic CRM. For creators who do high-ticket coaching, consulting, or 1:1 work alongside their content, the integrated CRM tracks deals and conversations. ConvertKit lacks CRM functionality.

**A/B testing depth:**
Brevo's A/B testing on Standard plan covers subject lines, content variants, and send times. ConvertKit's A/B testing is more limited.

## Deliverability

Both platforms maintain strong deliverability. ConvertKit's reputation for inbox placement is consistently strong — the platform built its early reputation partly on Patrick McKenzie's deliverability expertise.

Brevo's deliverability infrastructure is equally strong, with independent testing showing 95%+ inbox placement for properly authenticated senders. Both platforms support SPF, DKIM, DMARC, and one-click unsubscribe (required for Gmail/Yahoo compliance).

**Verdict: roughly equivalent.**

## Use Case Comparison

**Newsletter writer:**
ConvertKit if you value the Recommendations network and creator community. Brevo if you want SMS/WhatsApp options and lower costs.

**Course creator:**
ConvertKit if your funnel is purely email-based (lead magnet → nurture sequence → launch sequence → buyer onboarding). Brevo if you want to combine email with SMS reminders and use transactional email for course access notifications.

**Blogger with newsletter:**
ConvertKit for creator-specific templates and community. Brevo for cost savings and multi-channel options.

**Digital product seller:**
Brevo wins here. The combination of email automation + transactional email + lower costs serves digital product businesses better than ConvertKit's creator-only feature set.

**Coach or consultant:**
Brevo wins because of the integrated CRM and SMS capabilities. ConvertKit's pure email focus misses the multi-touch reality of high-ticket consulting.

**YouTube creator monetizing via email:**
ConvertKit for the creator community and recommendations. Brevo for cost savings on large lists.

## ConvertKit's Creator Network: Worth the Premium?

The Creator Network (Recommendations feature) is ConvertKit's strongest differentiator. When you opt in, ConvertKit shows your newsletter to other creators' subscribers at the moment they sign up, generating cross-promotional growth.

For newsletter writers in tight-knit communities (tech, indie hacking, design), this feature genuinely drives growth. Some creators report 5% to 15% of their new subscribers come from Recommendations.

The question: is this growth worth paying 3x to 5x more per month? For creators heavily reliant on referrals from other creators, possibly yes. For creators with strong external growth channels (YouTube, podcast, paid ads), probably not.

## Switching Considerations

If you're considering switching from ConvertKit to Brevo:

**The wins:**
- Immediate cost reduction (often 60% to 80%)
- Access to SMS and WhatsApp marketing
- Transactional email infrastructure
- Better automation depth

**The trade-offs:**
- No more Recommendations cross-promotion
- Less creator-specific templates and community
- Interface re-learning (Brevo's mental model is different)
- Subscriber migration takes 1 to 2 weeks for complete cutover

For most creators above 10,000 subscribers, the math strongly favors switching. For very early creators under 1,000 subscribers, ConvertKit's free plan is sufficient and the Recommendations network can accelerate growth.

## Migration Steps

If switching to Brevo:

1. **Export ConvertKit subscribers** with tag and segment data
2. **Set up Brevo lists and segments** matching your ConvertKit structure
3. **Import contacts** to Brevo (the CSV importer handles ConvertKit exports)
4. **Recreate critical sequences and automations** in Brevo's workflow builder
5. **Authenticate your sending domain** in Brevo (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
6. **Test a campaign** to a small segment
7. **Update embedded forms and landing pages** to use Brevo
8. **Cancel ConvertKit** once Brevo is producing equivalent results

Most creators complete the migration in 7 to 14 days.

## Final Verdict

**Choose ConvertKit if:**
- You value the Recommendations network for newsletter growth
- Your primary need is email-only marketing
- You're embedded in the creator community and brand recognition matters
- You're under 1,000 subscribers and the free plan covers you

**Choose Brevo if:**
- You're above 5,000 subscribers and cost matters
- You want SMS, WhatsApp, or multi-channel marketing
- You sell digital products needing transactional email
- You run a hybrid business (creator + service + ecommerce)
- You value automation depth and CRM integration

For roughly 70% of creators with growing lists, Brevo's pricing and feature breadth wins. The 30% who stay with ConvertKit do so primarily for the Recommendations network and creator community — both legitimate reasons.
