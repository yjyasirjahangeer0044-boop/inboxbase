# Brevo vs AWeber: Side-by-Side 2026 Comparison

AWeber is one of the oldest email marketing platforms still operating — founded in 1998, the platform predates most of the email marketing industry. That longevity means AWeber has stable infrastructure and a loyal customer base, but it also means the platform carries some legacy baggage: an interface that feels dated, automation that lags newer competitors, and pricing that hasn't aged well against modern alternatives like Brevo.

This comparison covers the pricing, features, automation capabilities, deliverability, and migration considerations for businesses choosing between Brevo and AWeber in 2026.

## What AWeber Does Well

To be fair, AWeber has real strengths:

**Customer support:**
AWeber's support team is consistently praised across reviews. Phone support is included on every paid plan (which Brevo reserves for Professional plan at $499/month). For users who prefer human assistance over self-service documentation, AWeber's support model is genuinely better at lower tiers.

**Reliability:**
27 years of operation means AWeber's infrastructure is rock-solid. Outages are rare. Email delivery is consistent. For risk-averse businesses, the proven track record matters.

**Beginner-friendly:**
The interface, while dated, is straightforward. New users can typically send a campaign within 15 to 20 minutes of signing up.

**Free plan with full features:**
AWeber's free plan (up to 500 subscribers) includes most of the platform's features rather than being a stripped-down preview. Smart approach for beginners who want to test thoroughly before paying.

**RSS-to-email:**
AWeber's RSS-to-email feature (auto-generating newsletters from blog RSS feeds) is well-implemented and widely used by bloggers. Brevo supports RSS but with less polish.

**Stability of pricing:**
AWeber hasn't significantly raised prices in years. Customers know what to expect month over month.

## What Brevo Does Better

**Pricing at every tier:**
Brevo's pricing advantage compounds as your list grows. The savings are substantial — often $20 to $80/month at modest scales, and into the hundreds at higher scales.

**Modern automation:**
Brevo's visual workflow builder is dramatically more capable than AWeber's. Conditional branching, multi-channel sequences, advanced triggers, and time-based delays all work more intuitively in Brevo. AWeber's automation feels like a feature added to a 2000s-era platform — because that's what it is.

**Multi-channel marketing:**
Brevo includes SMS, WhatsApp, web push, and live chat. AWeber is email + landing pages only. For modern multi-channel campaigns, AWeber simply doesn't have the infrastructure.

**Transactional email:**
Brevo includes transactional email APIs. AWeber doesn't offer transactional infrastructure — you'd need separate tools for order confirmations, password resets, etc.

**Modern interface:**
Brevo's UI is contemporary and clean. AWeber's interface feels like it hasn't been substantially redesigned in 5+ years — functional but visually dated.

**A/B testing:**
Brevo's A/B testing on Standard plan supports subject lines, content variants, and send times. AWeber's A/B testing is more limited.

**CRM features:**
Brevo includes a basic CRM. AWeber doesn't have CRM functionality.

**Free plan generosity:**
Brevo's free plan supports 100,000 subscribers. AWeber's caps at 500. For early-stage businesses, the difference is significant.

**API quality:**
Brevo's REST API is well-documented with SDKs in 7+ languages. AWeber's API exists but feels less polished and has fewer integrations.

## Automation: The Biggest Gap

This deserves its own section because the gap is large.

**AWeber automation:**
- Linear autoresponder sequences (welcome series, drip campaigns)
- Tag-based triggers
- Basic conditional logic
- Limited multi-step branching

**Brevo automation:**
- Visual workflow builder with unlimited branches
- Multi-channel sequences (email + SMS + WhatsApp in same workflow)
- Behavior-based triggers (page visits, link clicks, segment changes)
- Conditional delays and timing logic
- Goal tracking within workflows
- A/B testing within automation paths

For businesses that need modern automation — abandoned cart flows, behavior-triggered nurture sequences, multi-touch campaigns — AWeber feels constraining. Brevo handles these scenarios easily.

## Deliverability

Both platforms maintain strong deliverability infrastructure. AWeber's 27-year track record has built strong relationships with mailbox providers. Brevo's deliverability is equally strong with independent testing showing 95%+ inbox placement.

For properly authenticated sending (SPF, DKIM, DMARC configured), both platforms deliver well. **Roughly equivalent.**

## Customer Support Comparison

This is one area where AWeber genuinely wins for users who value human support:

**AWeber:**
- 24/7 phone, chat, and email support on every paid plan
- Live US-based support team
- Reputation for high-quality responses

**Brevo:**
- Email support on Free plan
- Chat on Starter and Standard
- Phone support only on Professional ($499/month)
- Dedicated account manager on Enterprise

For solo founders or small teams who prefer calling for help, AWeber's support model is more accessible. For self-sufficient users comfortable with documentation and chat, Brevo's support is fine and the platform's lower cost more than compensates.

## Integrations

AWeber has roughly 1,000 integrations through its app marketplace, leaning toward established platforms (Shopify, WordPress, Salesforce, ClickBank, Etsy).

Brevo has 150+ native integrations plus Zapier (5,000+ apps) plus a strong REST API. For most use cases, the integration coverage is comparable. AWeber edges out for some niche platforms; Brevo wins for API flexibility.

## Who Should Choose AWeber

AWeber makes sense if:
- You strongly value phone support at all price tiers
- You run a simple email marketing operation (newsletter + occasional broadcasts)
- You prefer established, conservative platforms over modern alternatives
- Your list is small (under 5,000) and price isn't a primary concern
- You use RSS-to-email heavily and want the most polished implementation

## Who Should Choose Brevo

Brevo makes sense if:
- You want modern automation capabilities
- You need SMS, WhatsApp, or multi-channel marketing
- You're cost-conscious as your list grows
- You require transactional email + marketing in one tool
- You want a contemporary interface and faster iteration
- You're building anything beyond basic email broadcasts

For roughly 80% of growing businesses, Brevo's combination of modern features and aggressive pricing wins decisively.

## Migration from AWeber to Brevo

If switching:

1. **Export AWeber subscribers** including tags and custom fields
2. **Set up Brevo lists** matching your AWeber list structure
3. **Import contacts** to Brevo (the importer handles AWeber CSV exports)
4. **Recreate autoresponders** as Brevo automations (the workflow builder is more powerful, so this is also an opportunity to improve)
5. **Authenticate your sending domain** in Brevo (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
6. **Test campaigns** to a small segment
7. **Update website forms** to point to Brevo
8. **Cancel AWeber** once Brevo is producing equivalent results

Most migrations complete in 1 to 3 weeks. The cost savings typically pay back the migration effort within 1 to 2 months.

## Final Verdict

AWeber is a stable, reliable platform with excellent customer support — but it's been left behind on automation, multi-channel marketing, and pricing. For new users in 2026, choosing AWeber over Brevo only makes sense if you specifically prioritize phone support over modern features.

**For most businesses, Brevo delivers more capability at significantly lower cost.** The modern automation alone is reason enough to choose Brevo if you need anything beyond basic email broadcasts.
