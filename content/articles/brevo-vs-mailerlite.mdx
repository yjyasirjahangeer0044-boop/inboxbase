# Brevo vs MailerLite: Which Wins for Growing Brands in 2026?

MailerLite has built a strong reputation among bloggers, creators, and small businesses for its clean interface and generous free plan. It's one of the closest competitors to Brevo on price, which makes this comparison genuinely interesting. But "close on price" doesn't mean "equal in capability." When you look at what each platform actually unlocks as your business grows, the picture shifts sharply in Brevo's favor.

If you're choosing between Brevo and MailerLite in 2026, this guide will walk through pricing, features, deliverability, automation depth, multi-channel reach, and the real-world scenarios where one beats the other. The short answer: MailerLite is fine for static newsletters under 1,000 subscribers. Brevo is built for businesses that plan to actually grow, sell, and engage customers across email, SMS, WhatsApp, and transactional flows.

## Pricing Compared Head-to-Head

Both platforms publish transparent pricing, but the structures differ. MailerLite charges by subscriber count. Brevo charges by emails sent, with unlimited contacts on every paid plan. This is a fundamental difference that compounds as you scale.

| Feature | Brevo Standard | MailerLite Growing Business |
|---------|---------------|------------------------------|
| **Monthly Price** | $18 | ~$18 (for 5,000 subscribers) |
| **Contacts** | Unlimited | 5,000 (then jumps) |
| **Emails Included** | 5,000/month | Unlimited |
| **SMS Marketing** | ✅ Built-in | ❌ Not available |
| **WhatsApp Campaigns** | ✅ Available | ❌ Not available |
| **Transactional Email API** | ✅ Included | ❌ Separate product |
| **Automation Workflows** | Unlimited steps | Limited on lower tiers |
| **A/B Testing** | ✅ Subject, content, send time | ✅ Subject and content |
| **Landing Pages** | ✅ Unlimited | ✅ Unlimited |
| **Send-Time Optimizer** | ✅ AI-powered | ❌ Manual scheduling |

On paper the entry pricing looks similar. But notice the contact limit. With MailerLite, the moment you cross 5,000 subscribers you jump to the next tier at ~$32/month. Cross 10,000 and you're at $50+. Brevo's $18 Standard plan supports unlimited contacts forever — you only pay more when your monthly email volume grows.

For a list growing from 5K to 25K subscribers over a year, MailerLite will roughly triple its monthly cost. Brevo stays flat unless you also triple your sending volume, which most lists don't.

## What MailerLite Does Better

To be fair, MailerLite has real strengths that explain its popularity with creators and small bloggers.

**Drag-and-drop editor polish.** MailerLite's editor feels modern and forgiving. Drag a block, drop it, edit inline. It's arguably the smoothest editing experience among budget email platforms. Brevo's editor is functional and capable but slightly more utilitarian.

**Generous free plan for tiny lists.** MailerLite's free plan supports 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. If you have 200 subscribers and send a weekly newsletter, you may never need to pay. Brevo's free plan caps at 300 emails/day but allows 100,000 contacts — so the trade-off depends on your usage pattern.

**Website builder and digital product selling.** MailerLite has expanded into hosting simple websites and selling digital downloads directly from its platform. For a solo creator who wants email + website + product sales in one tool, this matters. Brevo focuses on marketing and transactional messaging, leaving website building to specialized tools.

**Newsletter-focused simplicity.** If your entire business is a Substack-style newsletter and you have no plans to add ecommerce, SMS, transactional emails, or complex segmentation, MailerLite's narrower feature set means less to learn.

These are genuine advantages. If you're a hobbyist blogger with 500 subscribers and zero ambition to scale, MailerLite is a reasonable choice.

## What Brevo Does Better (and It's a Long List)

For everyone else, Brevo's advantages stack up quickly.

**Unlimited contacts on every paid plan.** This is the single biggest financial advantage. A coaching business growing from 5K to 50K subscribers on Brevo Standard pays $18/month the entire time (assuming sending stays within 5K/month). On MailerLite, that same growth pushes monthly cost past $100. Over three years of growth, the cost difference is thousands of dollars.

**SMS marketing built into the platform.** SMS open rates run 90%+ versus 25-30% for email. For ecommerce, course launches, restaurant promotions, and appointment-based businesses, SMS is no longer optional — it's essential. Brevo lets you send SMS from the same contact lists you use for email, with the same automation engine. MailerLite has no native SMS at all. You'd need a separate Twilio account, a separate workflow, and manual list syncing.

**WhatsApp Business campaigns.** Reaching customers on WhatsApp matters globally, especially in Latin America, India, Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and parts of Europe. Brevo supports WhatsApp templates and campaigns natively. MailerLite has no WhatsApp integration.

**Transactional email included.** Order confirmations, password resets, shipping notifications, account verifications — these emails matter more for retention than any newsletter. Brevo includes a powerful transactional API on every paid plan. With MailerLite you'd need a separate service like SendGrid or Postmark, adding $10-50/month and a second integration to maintain.

**Deeper automation logic.** Brevo workflows support branching by event, contact attribute, behavior score, and external data. You can trigger flows from website activity, purchase events, custom API calls, or scoring thresholds. MailerLite's automation has improved but still feels lighter — fine for "welcome series + abandoned cart" but limited for complex multi-step funnels.

**Built-in CRM and sales pipeline.** Brevo includes a free CRM with deal stages, contact timelines, and task management. For service businesses, agencies, and B2B, this consolidates email + sales tracking in one tool. MailerLite has no CRM functionality.

**Advanced segmentation.** Brevo supports nested AND/OR logic, behavioral segments, predictive segments on higher plans, and dynamic content based on contact attributes. MailerLite's segmentation is competent but flatter.

**AI features included.** Brevo's Standard plan includes AI-assisted subject lines, send-time optimization, and content suggestions. MailerLite has AI subject writing but less depth elsewhere.

## Deliverability Comparison

Deliverability is where many comparisons get hand-wavy. The honest truth: both Brevo and MailerLite have strong deliverability for shared-IP customers who follow basic hygiene rules (SPF, DKIM, DMARC, clean lists). Third-party email tester results typically show both platforms scoring in the 90-95% inbox placement range across major mailbox providers.

Where Brevo edges ahead: dedicated IP options on Professional and Enterprise plans, smart IP rotation logic that protects shared-pool reputation, and more aggressive proactive monitoring of sender behavior. For senders crossing 50,000+ emails/month, Brevo's infrastructure is purpose-built to scale without deliverability drops.

For senders under 5,000 emails/month with engaged lists, the practical difference is negligible. Both will land in the inbox if you do the basics right.

## Real-World Use Cases

**You're a solo blogger or newsletter writer with under 1,000 subscribers.** MailerLite's free plan is hard to beat. Brevo also has a free plan, but if you only send weekly broadcasts to a small list, MailerLite's polished editor and simpler interface may feel friendlier.

**You're a small ecommerce store selling physical or digital products.** Brevo wins clearly. You need abandoned cart automation, transactional emails for order confirmations, SMS for shipping updates, and segmentation by purchase behavior. MailerLite handles email but leaves SMS and transactional as separate problems.

**You're a coach, consultant, or course creator.** Brevo gives you email + CRM + landing pages + transactional + SMS in one tool. As your audience grows past 5K subscribers, the unlimited-contacts pricing saves you significant money. MailerLite would work initially but becomes expensive at scale.

**You run a SaaS or app business.** Brevo is the obvious choice — its transactional API handles your product-driven emails (welcome, password reset, billing, feature notifications) while the marketing side handles newsletters and lifecycle campaigns. MailerLite isn't built for this hybrid use case.

**You're a B2B agency managing client lists.** Brevo's CRM, multi-user accounts, and unlimited contacts make agency work practical. MailerLite has client management features but lacks the CRM depth.

## When to Choose MailerLite

Choose MailerLite if all of these apply: your list is under 5,000 subscribers, you only send broadcast newsletters (no complex automation), you have no need for SMS or WhatsApp, you don't send transactional emails, and you value editor polish over feature breadth. Hobby bloggers and small Substack-style publications often fit this profile.

## When to Choose Brevo (Most Growing Businesses)

Choose Brevo if any of these apply: you plan to grow past 5,000 contacts, you sell products or services online, you want SMS or WhatsApp now or in the future, you send transactional emails from a product or app, you need a CRM alongside email, you want sophisticated automation with branching logic, or you simply want predictable pricing as you scale.

For 70%+ of businesses making this decision in 2026, Brevo is the answer.

## Migration: Moving From MailerLite to Brevo

The migration path is straightforward. Most users complete it in a single afternoon.

1. **Export your MailerLite subscribers as CSV.** Go to Subscribers → Export. Include all fields and tags.
2. **Import into Brevo.** Use Contacts → Import → CSV. Map MailerLite's groups to Brevo's lists, and tags to Brevo attributes or list segments.
3. **Recreate your automation workflows in Brevo.** Welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement — rebuild each visually in Brevo's automation builder. Most workflows take 30-60 minutes each.
4. **Migrate transactional emails (if any).** Set up Brevo's transactional API in your app or website. Brevo's docs and SMTP relay make this painless.
5. **Authenticate your sending domain.** Add SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records pointing to Brevo. This protects deliverability during the switch.
6. **Run parallel sends for one week.** Send to a small segment from Brevo while MailerLite still handles the rest, comparing open rates and inbox placement.
7. **Cut over fully** and cancel MailerLite once Brevo metrics match or exceed.

## Final Verdict

MailerLite is a solid platform for a narrow audience: small newsletter writers who will never need SMS, transactional email, CRM, or advanced automation. For everyone else — and especially for businesses that plan to grow — Brevo is the smarter long-term choice. Unlimited contacts, built-in SMS and WhatsApp, transactional email included, deeper automation, and a free CRM all bundled at $18/month is value MailerLite simply doesn't match.

The unlimited-contacts pricing alone saves growing businesses thousands of dollars over a few years. Add the multi-channel features and you're getting a platform that handles email marketing, transactional messaging, SMS campaigns, customer relationship tracking, and landing pages from one dashboard.
